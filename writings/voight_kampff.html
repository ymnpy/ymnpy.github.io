voight-kampff test

People who know some details about me probably know that I feel attracted to cyperpunk themes. Themes where future described as and I have heard it in somewhere (probably a youtube video) high tech, low life. The daily life has all its interesting technological innovations, interesting gadgets or cyborg attachments to human beings etc. but on the other hand, love, sympathy to one other, moral values etc. are at the edge of dying. The tone is mostly gray, one might say I am basically describing a futuristic dystopian, fictional world and probably that would be true. Anyways, I read a famous about about this title, a book that later inspired a movie or maybe many movies. Its called Do androids dream of electrical sheep ? - by Philip K. Dick.

The book describes a timeline, a fictional world which is similar to what I have just wrote. A gray, futuristic, but kind of dying, dystopian world. For instance, in that fictional world many of the animals went extinct, reamining ones are highly valuable and people buy animals fake (electrical) or living as a status symbol, like todays phone brands etc. But more interestingly than that, there are androids, called replicants. They are called replicants, because, I assume they are almost identical to human beings. Hard to distinguish... That's where the story gets interesting. Bounty hunters, who work under goverment police stations and hunt down some of these androids apply a test to decide whether the subject is human or an android. Its called voight-kampff test.It basically detects, some emotional, empathic responses which occurs while giving answers to some odd questions asked by bounty-hunters (now thinking again, only one bounty hunter using this test in the book, maybe it differs from town to town, not sure but there is a pretty high possibility other bounty hunters might be using it as well).

I have found this technic interesting. Because both the book and the test puts the empathy at the core of their process. The test basically claims that, androids don't show any empathy, no emotional response seeing a dead goat (remember animals are rare in this fictional world) lying on their living room etc. To them, these are just some sort of facts, nothing to be surprised or sad. For humans its different, they give quick emotional response to those maybe through empathy or other means. How legit is this test in the real world I wonder. At first, it make sense not to expect any emotional response or empathy towards other living beings from robots. So this test can be a really good tool for detection, detection of false humans. But one might argue that those emotional responses such as blushing, fluctiation of the pupil etc. can be programmed into those robots, but I am not sure whether this is applicable or not. Maybe instruct robot to do those empathic responses whenever they hear a sentence including a harmful word and a living being ? I am not sure. Emotion and empathy, selfless moves... I feel like these are some great human features that cant be replicated fully. Dont sociopaths, who try to imitate other humans, seems odd to their close proximity ? I want to say they do, they cant fully, sincerely replicate those emotions. But I am not sure.

The book gets interesting where this empathic or emotional line between humans and androids gets distorted and kind of push you towards -what is the meaning of being human- not biologically but philosophically. If empathy, love for another, selfless acts for greater good, sacrificing yourself for a good cause are some of -being human features- then if one lose those does it mean he/she has also lost his/her humanity ? or atleast part of it ? and if an android show some of those features, sincerely, maybe through some machine learning algorithm, as it trains itself through many emotional experiences and re-calibrate its self-being-model or view-of-the-world model then maybe those features occur ? or maybe through a bug in its algorithm ? then can we say that this robot attained some portion of humanity ? I feel like when this happens or not even this, when they make humanoid robots with fluid movements and almost non-distinguishable human features people will feel empathy towards them as if they are their fellow human.

These are some raw thoughts came to my mind about the subject. But as I have mentioned before, empathy is an interesting subject which is strongly linked to humanity. Now it came to my mind, maybe its also shown in animals as well ? A quick check on the internet says they do. But for the sake of the argument I will stick to humans and end this by saying that just like now we are having difficulty to seperate between ai made videos or other media files and human made ones, maybe in the future we will feel difficulty of seperating humanoid androids and humans. Then maybe something like voight-kampff test will be useful in that scenario.
